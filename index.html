3. Into the Monsoon

Posted 16th of October 2019

Yesterday evening we were struck with the first real monsoon rain. It already rained twice before now, once two weeks ago and once two days ago at night, but it was nothing compared to tonight. It started raining lightly in the early evening but we still decided to go out for dinner because we had no food at home, and just when we started making our way back home in the evening, it started to rain again. Pascal asked whether we should stop somewhere and wait a little but I wanted to just go home as fast as possible before the rain would get really heavy. Well, that didn't work out, because it went from light rain to raining buckets within two minutes, and by the time we came home after about ten minutes, we were both completely soaked. Miraculously, all the stuff that I had inside my fanny pack was still perfectly dry, which makes me very happy because it means I won't have to worry about my valuables when I get into the monsoon again.
So when I went to work today, I knew all the dirt roads would be completely muddy and very difficult to manage by bike, so I took another road to SVARAM which I had wanted to try out anyways. Luckily, that road is mainly asphalt and stone and it's also slightly shorter than the dirt road that I used for the past few days so that won't be a big problem during the monsoon. Some parts are still dirt road and there were puddles the size of ponds and generally a lot of muddy patches to manage, but it all works fine.
Generally, as long as I am not on a dirt road and have sufficient light and my valuables safely stored, I really love going through the monsoon as it's just an insane exerience compared to the rain we have in Austria. It's also still very warm and the rainwater is at least lukewarm so it actually feels nice.
Oh and unsurprisingly we had power cuts for most of the night because of the thunderstorms but it was okay since it still worked just long enough to charge our phones and due to the rain it was cool enough to not die without a fan.

4. Files, files, and more files

I started working on cleaning up and organizing our server yesterday. Basically, Aurelio told me last week that all the SVARAM files, pictures, recordings etc. are stored on one server which is a huge mess and is basically impossible to properly work with. I told him that I enjoy organizing and sorting stuff, so him and Ralf decided to put me to it. Tuesday afternoon, Ralf and I brainstormed on which categories of folders and subfolders we'd need and what should go where, and then I began with the image folder on our server and all its sub- subsub- subsubsub- and subsubsubsub-folders. First, I kind of despaired because it really is a huge mess and taking all of that and moving everything from wherever it is to where it should be would take ages. However, when I came to work yesterday and looked at it again, I decided that it would be much more efficient and also less risky to start from scratch. So instead of taking all the current folders and re-organizing them, I made my own ones (those that Ralf and I decided would be needed) and started going from folder to folder, copying everything, and pasting it into my corresponding new folder. That way, the current system stays how it is for now and when I'm done we can either just delete all the old folders, or if they decide that some things were better about the old system, it's all still there.
As soon as I started working that way, it all went very smoothly and I was able to work very efficiently. It's still a whole lot of work and will take quite some time, but now I'm actually enjoying it.
